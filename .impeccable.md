## Design Context

### Users

ShellTeam is for technical founders and software developers who operate their own
Linux box and want to direct several coding agents from a browser, often from a
phone as well as a desktop. Their core jobs are to start and steer agent work,
inspect output, move between terminal/files/browser views, and recover quickly
when credentials, services, or optional modules are unavailable.

### Brand Personality

Restrained, assured, and meticulously crafted. ShellTeam should feel like a
serious operator's instrument: calm under pressure, direct without being cold,
and premium through precision rather than decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction

Keep the existing dark, compact cockpit and its established tokens and
components. Favor clear hierarchy, quiet surfaces, strong typography, and rare
purposeful accents. Avoid generic dashboard cards, decorative gradients, visual
noise, and one-off widgets that diverge from the main rendering pipeline.

### Design Principles

1. Keyboard and touch are first-class inputs; every action must work at a 390 px
   phone viewport and without a pointer.
2. Preserve context and momentum: state changes, errors, and recovery actions
   should be explicit and actionable without blocking unrelated work.
3. Reuse the cockpit's existing components, tokens, and rendering pipeline.
4. Prefer fewer, clearer controls and progressive disclosure over option density.
5. Accessibility and resilience are release criteria, not post-launch polish.
